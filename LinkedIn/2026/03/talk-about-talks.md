Yesterday I went meta and out of my comfort zone 😅

At Novanet we have our internal community evenings every third week where we share knowledge. Since conference season is upon us — which is honestly just a long, continuous, never-ending, 365-days-a-year season at this point — I did a talk on how I prepare for talks, build them, and present them.

A few things I think actually matter:

🎭 **There are no rules.** Own your style. Don't let anyone tell you how you should present.

📖 **You're telling a story.** Not showcasing your expertise. If your message is good, credibility is a by-product — not the goal.

� **Keep it light.** Humor sets the tone — it makes the room comfortable, lowers the guard, and signals that this is going to be a good time. Nobody learns well when they're tense.

🖼️ **Pictures over text.** If you're making the audience read, just send a Word document instead. Save the bullet points for your speaker notes.

💡 **What's obvious to you isn't obvious to everyone else.** Say the thing that feels too basic. That's usually the thing people needed to hear.

🗂️ **Treat your slides as a library.** Keep everything organized and think about reusability. A slide from two years ago might be exactly what you need next month.

Conference season — ready or not 🚀
